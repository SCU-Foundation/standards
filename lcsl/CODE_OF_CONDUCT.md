# Code of Conduct — Lattice Certification & Safety Layer (LCSL)

**Standard/Unit:** Lattice Certification & Safety Layer (LCSL)  
**Version:** v1.0  
**Steward:** SCU Foundation  
**Research Body:** Lattice Research Institute (LRI)  
**Repository:** REPO: Standards — /lcsl/

---

## Purpose

This Code of Conduct establishes the behavioural expectations for all contributors participating in the stewardship, maintenance, or discussion of this standard, architecture unit, or repository.  
It ensures that all work within the SCU ecosystem remains neutral, safe, professional, and aligned with the constitutional trust, safety, and certification principles defined by the SCU Foundation.

---

## Guiding Principles

Contributors MUST:

- act with professionalism, respect, and integrity  
- maintain neutrality and avoid conflicts of interest  
- uphold the trust, safety, certification, and capability‑boundary principles defined by the SCU Foundation  
- avoid introducing proprietary, product‑specific, or commercial bias  
- respect the governance authority of the SCU Foundation  
- respect the research independence of the Lattice Research Institute (LRI)  
- ensure all contributions align with the canonical repository structure and governed update sequences  
- follow the SCU Foundation’s interoperability, safety, certification, and boundary rules  

---

## Expected Behaviour

Participants MUST:

- engage constructively and respectfully in discussions  
- provide clear, evidence‑based reasoning for proposals  
- follow the SCU Foundation’s change‑management and review processes  
- maintain confidentiality for any non‑public governance deliberations  
- avoid personal attacks, harassment, or discriminatory behaviour  
- avoid misrepresentation of the standard, architecture, or governance model  
- collaborate in good faith and avoid obstructive behaviour  

---

## Unacceptable Behaviour

The following behaviours are prohibited:

- harassment, discrimination, or abusive conduct  
- introducing proprietary or commercial influence into normative content  
- attempting to bypass governance or change‑management processes  
- misusing the standard or architecture for endorsement or promotional purposes  
- submitting misleading, harmful, or intentionally ambiguous proposals  
- violating the licensing terms or misrepresenting attribution  
- disregarding canonical update sequences or governance decisions

## Enforcement

The SCU Foundation is responsible for:

- interpreting this Code of Conduct  
- reviewing alleged violations  
- determining appropriate actions or sanctions  
- maintaining the neutrality and integrity of the ecosystem  

The Foundation may:

- issue warnings  
- request revisions or retractions  
- restrict participation  
- remove contributors from governance processes  

All decisions are final within the scope of standards stewardship.
